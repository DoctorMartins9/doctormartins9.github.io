Enrico Martini is a postdoctoral researcher working on computational human motion modeling, multimodal sensor fusion, and optimization-based estimation for real-time human modeling.

His research spans markerless human pose estimation, gait analysis, sparse body-worn sensing, and safe human-robot interaction. He builds model-based and data-driven methods that remain deployable on edge hardware and validates them with synthetic experiments and human data.

Over the last five years, he has authored journal and conference publications across signal processing, robotics, and biomedical engineering venues. He previously held a visiting scholar position at the University of Pennsylvania.
